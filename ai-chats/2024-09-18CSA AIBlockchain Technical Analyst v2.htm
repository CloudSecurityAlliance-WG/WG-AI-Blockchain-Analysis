Emerging AI Regulations in Healthcare (2024)

The use of AI in healthcare is growing rapidly, with applications ranging from diagnostics to personalized medicine. However, this rise has prompted regulatory bodies to develop new frameworks aimed at mitigating the potential risks AI poses to patient safety, data privacy, and equity. Here's an in-depth look at the evolving landscape of AI regulations in healthcare:

1. Algorithmic Bias and Discrimination

A key concern is that AI models trained on biased or incomplete datasets could exacerbate healthcare disparities. AI systems may produce discriminatory outcomes by perpetuating biases present in their training data, affecting diagnostic accuracy, treatment recommendations, or healthcare access across different demographics. New regulations aim to ensure that AI tools used in healthcare are transparent and free from bias:

  • The HHS Office of Civil Rights recently introduced regulations targeting algorithmic discrimination in AI. These rules focus on ensuring AI systems do not disadvantage vulnerable populations​(

Deloitte United States

).

  • The FDA has issued guidance on AI and machine learning (ML) technologies in medical devices, emphasizing the need for ongoing monitoring to ensure fairness and transparency​(

Deloitte United States

).

2. Transparency and Explainability

AI’s complexity often leads to a "black box" problem, where decisions made by algorithms are difficult to understand or explain. This is problematic in healthcare, where clinical accountability and patient safety are critical. New policies are being enacted to promote transparency:

  • The FDA’s proposed rule mandates that AI/ML tools embedded in medical devices must be transparent regarding how they arrive at decisions, particularly in life-critical areas like diagnostics and treatment​(

Deloitte United States

).

  • The Office of the National Coordinator for Health IT (ONC) has also called for more transparency in AI systems, requiring healthcare IT developers to disclose details about AI algorithms and their performance​(

Deloitte United States

).

3. AI Governance and Oversight

There is increasing recognition of the need for robust governance frameworks to oversee the deployment of AI in healthcare:

  • Healthcare organizations are being urged to establish AI governance structures, including bias audits, ethics reviews, and real-time monitoring of AI applications​(

Deloitte United States

  • The U.S. White House has also prioritized AI governance, proposing national standards for AI development and deployment that would impact healthcare sectors, particularly around privacy and safety​(

GeBBS Healthcare Solutions

The FDA has established a dedicated framework for the regulation of AI/ML in medical devices, requiring that these technologies undergo stringent reviews both before and after deployment:

  • The FDA has also emphasized post-market surveillance of AI-enabled devices, requiring developers to monitor outcomes and ensure AI performance does not degrade or deviate from its original intent​(
